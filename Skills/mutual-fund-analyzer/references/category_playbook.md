# SEBI Category Playbook (India)

Maps SEBI's 2018+ categorization to (a) the correct benchmark, (b) the expected portfolio signature, and (c) the key scoring signal per category.

**How to use:** Identify the fund's SEBI category → find the section below → confirm benchmark fit → know what the portfolio *should* look like → apply the right metric.

---

## Equity categories

### Multi-cap
**Mandate:** ≥75% equity; minimum 25% each in large, mid, small cap (post-Sep-2020 SEBI rule).
**Benchmark:** Nifty 500 Multicap 50:25:25 TRI.
**Key signal:** Verify genuine spread across all three cap tiers. A multi-cap running 70%+ large-cap is in violation. Small-cap liquidity deteriorates fast as AUM grows.

### Flexi-cap
**Mandate:** ≥65% equity, no cap-tier constraint.
**Benchmark:** Nifty 500 TRI (sometimes BSE 500 TRI).
**Key signal:** Track the average cap tilt across multiple factsheets — the freedom is the point, but it makes benchmark gaming easy. Funds benchmarked to Nifty 50 are flattering themselves.

### Large-cap
**Mandate:** ≥80% in top-100 stocks by market cap.
**Benchmark:** Nifty 100 TRI or BSE 100 TRI.
**Key signal:** Hardest category to generate alpha — the index is efficient. Consistent net outperformance of even 100–150 bps over Nifty 100 TRI is real work. Default presumption favours a low-cost index fund unless the active fund clears a high bar. TER >1.5% direct is hard to justify here.

### Large & mid-cap
**Mandate:** ≥35% large-cap and ≥35% mid-cap.
**Benchmark:** Nifty Large Midcap 250 TRI.
**Key signal:** Mid-cap sleeve is where alpha typically comes from. Check mid-cap name quality and whether it is concentrated or diversified.

### Mid-cap
**Mandate:** ≥65% in stocks ranked 101–250 by market cap.
**Benchmark:** Nifty Midcap 150 TRI.
**Key signal:** Drawdown discipline matters more than in large-cap — midcap drawdowns are deeper. AUM >₹30,000 cr = likely a closet large-mid fund (can't take meaningful positions in the small mid-cap universe).

### Small-cap
**Mandate:** ≥65% in stocks ranked 251+ by market cap.
**Benchmark:** Nifty Smallcap 250 TRI or BSE 250 SmallCap TRI.
**Key signal:** Capacity is the dominant issue. Funds pausing lump-sum flows = integrity, not weakness. Drawdowns of 35–50% in a stress year are normal; penalise only if materially worse than the index.

### ELSS
**Mandate:** ≥80% equity, 3-year lock-in, 80C deduction (old regime).
**Benchmark:** Usually Nifty 500 TRI; sometimes Nifty 50 TRI.
**Key signal:** Treat as a flexi-cap with a lock-in. The lock-in helps the manager (no panic redemptions), so the fund should be slightly more aggressive. Do not award bonus points for the tax benefit — that is a category feature.

### Sectoral / Thematic
**Mandate:** ≥80% in a stated sector or theme.
**Benchmark:** The sector index (e.g., Nifty IT TRI, Nifty Bank TRI).
**Key signal:** Tactical bet, not a core holding. Even an excellent pharma fund is wrong for an investor with 30% pharma elsewhere. Suitability framing dominates — flag this for the Suitability Analyst.

### Focused
**Mandate:** Maximum 30 stocks, ≥65% equity.
**Benchmark:** Varies by AMC; Nifty 500 TRI common.
**Key signal:** Top-10 weight will naturally be 50–60%. Concentration is the feature. Key metric: is the concentration paying off in alpha? If not, the investor is carrying idiosyncratic risk for nothing.

### Dividend yield / Value / Contra
**Mandate:** Style-defined.
**Benchmark:** Nifty 500 TRI or a style index (e.g., Nifty Dividend Opportunities 50 TRI for dividend yield).
**Key signal:** Style consistency is the entire point. Use holdings to verify — a "value" fund holding HDFC AMC at 50x earnings is not a value fund. Style underperformance during off-style periods is expected; it is a sizing issue, not a sell signal.

## Debt categories

Three things drive all debt analysis: **duration profile** (matched to category), **credit quality** (matched to risk appetite), **YTM vs category median** (flag for hidden risk).

### Overnight
Duration: 1 day. Benchmark: CRISIL Overnight Index.
**Key signal:** Lowest possible TER, AAA-only paper. No surprises.

### Liquid
Duration: ≤91 days. Benchmark: CRISIL Liquid Debt Index.
**Key signal:** Stick to highest credit only. The 2018–2020 episodes (DHFL, Essel) showed what yield-chasing costs in liquid funds.

### Ultra short / Low duration / Money market
Duration: 3 months – 1 year. Benchmark: CRISIL-published category indices.
**Key signal:** YTM within 50 bps of category median. Outliers typically have lower-rated paper.

### Short / Medium / Long duration
Duration buckets: 1–3Y / 3–4Y / 7Y+ respectively. Benchmark: corresponding CRISIL bond indices.
**Key signal:** Modified duration must match the category label. A "short duration" fund running 3.5Y modified duration is hiding duration risk.

### Corporate bond
**Mandate:** ≥80% in AA+ and above.
**Benchmark:** CRISIL Corporate Bond Composite Index.
**Key signal:** True high-grade discipline; YTM typically government bond + 50–100 bps.

### Credit risk
**Mandate:** ≥65% in AA and below.
**Benchmark:** CRISIL Credit Risk Bond Index.
**Key signal:** Genuinely risk-on. Issuer concentration matters more than the rating bucket — a "diversified" AA portfolio with three issuers at 25%+ each is dangerous. Remember the Franklin Templeton 2020 freeze.

### Banking & PSU
**Mandate:** ≥80% in bank/PSU/public financial institution paper.
**Benchmark:** CRISIL Banking and PSU Debt Index.
**Key signal:** Typically AAA-heavy. Check credit-quality discipline and modest duration.

### Gilt / Gilt with 10-year duration
**Mandate:** ≥80% government securities; 10-year version targets constant 10Y duration.
**Benchmark:** CRISIL Dynamic Gilt Index / CRISIL 10-year Gilt Index.
**Key signal:** Pure interest-rate play. No credit risk. Duration discipline is the only variable.

### Dynamic bond
**Mandate:** Duration at manager's discretion.
**Benchmark:** CRISIL Dynamic Bond Index.
**Key signal:** Most manager-dependent debt category. Check whether duration calls have actually worked across rate cycles, not just the most recent one.

## Hybrid categories

### Conservative hybrid
Mandate: 10–25% equity / 75–90% debt. Suitable for short-to-medium horizons.

### Balanced hybrid / Aggressive hybrid
Balanced: 40–60% equity. Aggressive: 65–80% equity.
**Key signal:** Aggressive hybrids often run ~75% equity — effectively diluted equity funds. Compare risk-adjusted return to a pure equity fund of equivalent style.

### Multi-asset allocation
**Mandate:** ≥10% in each of ≥3 asset classes.
**Key signal:** Genuine diversification across equity, debt, and gold/commodity, not just a tilt.

### Equity savings
**Mandate:** Equity (≥65%) + arbitrage + debt.
**Key signal:** Net equity exposure post-hedging typically 30–40%. Tax-treated as equity.

### Arbitrage
**Mandate:** ≥65% cash-futures arbitrage.
**Key signal:** Steady, low-volatility return tracking short-term debt yields. Equity taxation is the main draw.

### Dynamic asset allocation / Balanced advantage (BAF)
**Key signal:** Many BAFs are effectively closet 65% equity funds with a model-driven marketing story. Check whether the dynamic allocation model has actually worked across cycles.

## Solution-oriented

### Retirement / Children's fund
Lock-in: 5 years or until retirement/child majority.
**Key signal:** Suitability framing dominates. The lock-in is significant.

### Index fund / ETF
**Key signal:** Tracking error and TER, full stop. Outperformance = tracking failure. For ETFs, also check trading volumes and bid-ask spreads — illiquid ETFs cost the investor on entry and exit in ways TER doesn't show.

### Fund of funds (FoF)
**Key signal:** Analyse the underlying fund(s) first, then add the FoF wrapper cost. Wrapper is hard to justify unless the underlying is otherwise inaccessible (e.g., overseas funds for retail Indian investors).

## Quick-reference penalty table

| Pattern | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Large-cap active failing to beat Nifty 100 TRI net of cost over 5Y | Heavy |
| Index fund TE >0.30% (large-cap) or >0.50% (mid/small) | Heavy |
| Debt fund YTM >150 bps above category median | Heavy (credit risk) |
| Single-issuer >10% in any debt fund | Heavy |
| Top-1 stock >10% in non-focused equity | Moderate-heavy |
| Style drift evident across 6+ months of holdings | Heavy |
| TER >2% Direct in any category | Heavy |
| AUM <₹100 cr beyond 3 years post-launch | Moderate |
| Manager change in last 12 months | Note as data caveat; cap People pillar |

## Cross-cutting reminders

- Beating the category median is necessary but not sufficient. Beating an honest, low-cost benchmark is the real bar.
- After-fee comparison is mandatory. 1% gross alpha at 1.5% TER = negative net alpha for the investor.
- Risk-free rate reference: prevailing 91-day T-bill or 1Y G-Sec yield. Use latest available.
