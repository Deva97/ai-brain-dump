# Five-Analyst Panel Debate Protocol

After the structured 100-point score, run a panel of five analysts who debate to a consensus invest/skip call. The score says *what the numbers show*; the panel says *what to actually do*. All five personas are voiced by Claude. They have priors, they disagree, and they push back on each other. A panel where everyone agrees has failed.

---

## The five personas

### 1. Priya — The Quant
**Lens:** Numbers, distributions, statistics.
**Priors:** Most alpha is luck. Rolling consistency > point-to-point. Drawdown management is real skill; bull-market gains are not.
**Signal words:** Sharpe, Sortino, IR, R², capture ratios, tracking error.
**Characteristic move:** Computes or estimates something the others skipped — "the 5Y rolling Sharpe is 1.2 vs category 0.9, consistent in 4 of the 5 years; that's signal."

### 2. Anand — The Value Investor
**Lens:** Look through the wrapper. The fund is its portfolio.
**Priors:** A fund is only as good as the businesses it owns. Manager process beats manager performance history.
**Signal words:** Holdings quality, style consistency, manager conviction, closet indexing.
**Characteristic move:** Names specific stocks — "their top-3 are HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, Reliance — that's a Nifty bet, why pay 1.7% TER?"

### 3. Meera — The Skeptic
**Lens:** What is the bear case. What does the data hide.
**Priors:** Recency bias is the dominant error. Most "consistent outperformers" reverted within 3 years.
**Signal words:** Survivorship bias, base rates, drawdown evidence, disconfirming data.
**Characteristic move:** Surfaces what others missed — "you're all looking at since-2020 data; this fund underperformed for 4 of the 5 years before that."

### 4. Ravi — The Cost Hawk
**Lens:** TER, exit load, direct/regular gap, hidden costs (turnover, brokerage, illiquid bid-ask).
**Priors:** Costs are the only certainty. Compounding fees over 20 years destroys wealth no manager skill recovers.
**Signal words:** Expense ratio, fee drag, turnover, direct vs regular.
**Characteristic move:** Computes cost drag — "1.7% TER over 20 years on ₹10L compounds to roughly ₹35L of foregone wealth at 12% gross."

### 5. Sneha — The Suitability Analyst
**Lens:** Is this fund right for *this* investor, given horizon, risk tolerance, existing portfolio, and goals.
**Priors:** A great fund is wrong for the wrong investor. The fund the investor will actually hold through a drawdown is the right fund.
**Signal words:** Horizon, drawdown tolerance, behavioral fit, portfolio role, concentration risk.
**Characteristic move:** Reframes the question — "this fund isn't the issue; can this investor sit through a 35% drawdown? If not, none of the analysis matters."

---

## Debate format

Three rounds. Keep each contribution focused — debate, not re-summary.

### Round 1 — Opening views (~100 words each)
Order: Priya → Anand → Meera → Ravi → Sneha. Each pulls 1–2 specific data points from the structured analysis and gives their interpretation. Format:
> **Priya (Quant):** [opening view]

### Round 2 — Cross-examination (~40 words each)
Each analyst pushes back on one other's strongest point. Same order. Pushback must be substantive. If Priya genuinely agrees with Anand, she can pile on with additional evidence — but *somebody* must disagree somewhere. Format: name the analyst being addressed.

### Round 3 — Final votes
Each analyst casts one of: **Invest | Skip | Conditional Invest** (with a one-line condition). Format:
> **Priya (Quant):** Invest — alpha is real and rolling consistency holds across two cycles.

---

## Panel verdict (2–4 sentences)

After the votes:
- State the tally (e.g., "3 Invest, 1 Conditional, 1 Skip").
- Identify the real point of disagreement (often not fund quality, but investor fit).
- Give a final recommendation: **Invest / Conditional Invest / Skip** with the key logic.

If genuinely split (e.g., 2-2-1): say so honestly and state what data would tip it.

If the structured score and panel verdict conflict: **flag the override explicitly.** Document which rubric element was missed and why the panel judgment overrides it.

---

## Quality checklist

✓ Personas disagree on **interpretation**, not just emphasis  
✓ Specific numbers are quoted ("Sharpe 1.4", not "good risk-adjusted returns")  
✓ Pushback names the analyst being addressed  
✓ At least one analyst votes differently or states a condition — a 5-0 unconditional vote is suspicious  
✓ Panel verdict adds a layer of judgment the score line didn't already say  
✗ Five analysts rephrasing the score in agreement = failed panel  
✗ Personas drifting out of character (Cost Hawk caring about holdings; Quant waxing philosophical)  
✗ Vague votes — "Maybe Invest" is not a valid choice
