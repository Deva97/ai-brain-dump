# 100-Point Scoring Rubric — Medalist Edition

Seven pillars totalling 100 points. Score each independently, sum, then apply the Net Alpha Filter before assigning a verdict tier.

---

## Verdict Tiers

| Tier | Score | Signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Gold** | 82–100 | High-conviction outperformer. Exceptional People, Process, Parent. |
| **Silver** | 68–81 | Likely outperformer. Solid across most pillars; fee load justified by demonstrated or expected alpha. |
| **Bronze** | 55–67 | Marginal edge. One or two pillar weaknesses; worth holding, not leading with. |
| **Neutral** | 40–54 | No material advantage over peers or an equivalent index fund. |
| **Negative** | <40 | Material flaws. Expected to underperform after fees. Avoid adding; consider exiting. |

Scores in the **65–70 band** are judgment calls — let the panel debate tip between Bronze and Silver.

---

## Net Alpha Filter (applied after summing pillar scores)

Cost is not scored as a standalone pillar. It is filtered through a net-alpha lens after summing pillars.

**Step 1** — Estimate gross alpha from Pillar 2 + Pillar 3 combined (out of 35):

| Combined score | Gross alpha estimate |
|---|---|
| 30–35 | >2% annualized |
| 22–29 | 1–2% |
| 15–21 | <1%; borderline |
| <15 | Negligible or negative |

**Step 2** — Subtract the fund's direct-plan TER.

**Step 3:**

| Net alpha situation | Action |
|---|---|
| Clearly positive (>0.5% after TER) | Verdict tier stands. |
| Near zero (0–0.5%) | Flag. Check whether strong People/Process/Parent justify current tier. |
| Negative after TER | Downgrade verdict one tier. Document explicitly. |
| TER in cheapest quartile | No bonus. Note cost efficiency in prose. |

**Passive funds:** Replace gross alpha estimation with a TE-to-cost check. Rising TE with flat TER = passive mandate failure.

---

## Analyst Conviction Level

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **High** | People ≥14/17, Process ≥12/15, Parent ≥10/13. Track record spans at least one full cycle including a drawdown and recovery. |
| **Medium** | Mixed qualitative signals. At least one of People/Process/Parent is uncertain or underinformed. |
| **Low** | Major qualitative gaps: manager change in last 12 months, AMC ownership change, mandate shift, or insufficient data on People/Process/Parent. |

**Conviction caps the verdict:**
- Low → Maximum verdict Bronze, regardless of numerical score. Flag the cap.
- High in 65–70 gray zone → Tips to Silver.
- Medium → No cap, but flag the uncertain pillar.

## Pillar 1 — Mandate and Benchmark Fit (8 pts)

| Score | When |
|---|---|
| 8 | Category, benchmark, and portfolio fully aligned. Benchmark is TRI-based and correct for the SEBI category. No style drift. |
| 6–7 | Aligned with a minor note (e.g., flexi-cap with consistent large-cap tilt — acceptable if transparent). |
| 3–5 | Visible mismatch: midcap benchmarked to Nifty 500; flexi-cap running >70% large-cap for years; style label contradicted by holdings. |
| 1–2 | Material mismatch — fund is effectively in a different category from its label. |
| 0 | Fund is misrepresenting itself. |

**Passive:** Score reflects whether the fund tracks the exact stated index with no proxy substitute.

---

## Pillar 2 — Return Quality (15 pts)

Weight rolling and SIP returns more heavily than trailing point-to-point. Compare to (a) benchmark TRI and (b) category median.

| Score | When |
|---|---|
| 13–15 | Beats benchmark TRI in 70%+ of 3Y rolling windows over last 5Y. SIP returns top-quartile. Outperformance distributed across years. |
| 10–12 | Beats benchmark TRI in 55–70% of 3Y rolling windows. Above-median SIP returns. |
| 7–9 | Roughly tracks benchmark net of cost. Rolling outperformance <55%. Around category median. |
| 3–6 | Lags benchmark consistently or narrow single-cycle outperformance. Below-median over 5Y. |
| 0–2 | Persistent underperformance vs both benchmark and category median on a rolling basis. |

**Passive — Tracking Quality:** 13–15 if TE consistently <0.10% (large-cap) / <0.30% (mid-small). 7–9 if TE is 0.20–0.40%. ≤6 if persistent TE >0.50%.

---

## Pillar 3 — Risk-adjusted Quality (20 pts)

**Active equity/hybrid weights:** Sharpe/Sortino vs median: 6 pts | Alpha at acceptable R²: 5 pts | Max drawdown + capture ratios: 6 pts | Information ratio: 3 pts.

| Score | When |
|---|---|
| 18–20 | Top-quartile Sharpe and Sortino. Positive alpha >1.5% at R² >0.80. Max drawdown materially better than benchmark in stress windows. Downside capture <85%. IR >0.5. |
| 14–17 | Above-median risk-adjusted metrics. Modest positive alpha. Drawdowns roughly in line with benchmark. |
| 10–13 | Around category median. Alpha indistinguishable from zero. |
| 5–9 | Below-median. Excess volatility for the return, or deeper drawdowns than the category. |
| 0–4 | High-risk, low-reward. Drawdowns materially worse than benchmark. Negative alpha. |

**Debt:** Replace Jensen's alpha with risk-adjusted yield (yield above benchmark / std dev). Weight max drawdown and credit-event behaviour heavily. Gilt funds: only duration-adjusted return matters.

**Passive:** Collapse to 12 pts; Pillar 2 absorbs remaining 8. Only TE stability and cost precision matter.

---

## Pillar 4 — Portfolio Quality (12 pts)

### Concentration and construction (6 pts)

| Score | When |
|---|---|
| 5–6 | Top-10 weight appropriate for the category. Single-stock <7% in diversified equity. Sector bets are deliberate and consistent with the stated process. Cash is intentional and disclosed. |
| 3–4 | Mild concentration concern: one oversize position, one unexplained sector tilt, or cash level unclear. |
| 1–2 | Top-1 stock >10% in a non-focused fund; or sector concentration >35% without a stated thematic mandate; or persistent unexplained cash >15%. |
| 0 | Portfolio is dangerously concentrated or incoherent with the mandate. |

**Debt substitutes:** Credit-quality breakup (AAA/AA/below-AA/SOV/Cash %) + modified duration vs category norm + issuer concentration. Single-issuer >10% in a credit fund = 0 pts on this sub-pillar.

### Style consistency (3 pts)

| Score | When |
|---|---|
| 3 | Holdings match the stated investment style across at least 3 consecutive factsheets. No drift. |
| 1–2 | Some drift visible — a growth fund holding deep cyclicals, a value fund holding expensive quality. Possibly tactical; possibly mandate creep. |
| 0 | Systematic style drift. The fund is not what it says it is. |

### Research team coverage (3 pts)

| Score | When |
|---|---|
| 3 | Dedicated research team with coverage matching the mandate (e.g., small-cap fund with small-cap researchers). Output is visible in portfolio decisions. |
| 1–2 | Research exists but is generic or stretched across too many mandates. |
| 0 | No visible research infrastructure. PM is effectively doing all the analysis. |

## Pillar 5 — People (17 pts)

### Manager track record (8 pts)

| Score | When |
|---|---|
| 7–8 | PM has managed this fund ≥5 years. Track record spans a full market cycle including at least one material drawdown and recovery. Outperformance is attributable to the current manager with high confidence. |
| 5–6 | PM tenure 3–5 years. Some cycle evidence but not a complete cycle. |
| 3–4 | PM tenure 1–3 years. Limited cycle evidence. Track record partially attributable to predecessors. |
| 1–2 | PM tenure <1 year, or significant strategy shift under new manager. Historical numbers belong to a different manager. |
| 0 | No identifiable PM, or a recent change so material that the track record is entirely non-attributable. |

If manager change in last 12 months: cap entire People pillar at 9/17.

### Team depth and bench (5 pts)

| Score | When |
|---|---|
| 4–5 | Named deputy or co-manager. Succession is plausible and has precedent at the AMC. Bench depth extends below the named PM. |
| 2–3 | Team exists but bench is thin. Succession possible but uncertain. |
| 0–1 | Key-person risk. If the PM leaves, the fund has no clear successor and the track record goes with them. |

### Incentive alignment (4 pts)

| Score | When |
|---|---|
| 3–4 | Co-investment by the PM disclosed. Compensation structured to align with long-term fund performance. |
| 1–2 | Standard industry structure. No evidence of misalignment, but no disclosed co-investment. |
| 0 | Evidence of misaligned incentives (e.g., PM has heavy AUM pressure with no performance link). |

---

## Pillar 6 — Process (15 pts)

### Alpha source clarity (5 pts)

| Score | When |
|---|---|
| 4–5 | Specific, repeatable edge articulated (e.g., "earnings-quality screening before valuation"). Portfolio reflects this articulation. Differentiated from standard screener output. |
| 2–3 | Process sounds reasonable but lacks a differentiating logic. Generic "bottom-up, quality-focused, long-term." |
| 0–1 | Generic, incoherent, or inconsistent with the actual portfolio. |

### Portfolio construction discipline (5 pts)

| Score | When |
|---|---|
| 4–5 | Position sizes reflect conviction. Additions and exits consistent with stated thesis. Turnover coherent with strategy. |
| 2–3 | Mostly disciplined but with episodes of drift — opportunistic sector bet, size tilt, chasing a sector rally. |
| 0–1 | High unexplained turnover, persistent style drift, or portfolio assembled reactively. |

### Risk management rigor (5 pts)

| Score | When |
|---|---|
| 4–5 | Drawdown management systematic and documented across ≥2 distinct stress periods. Downside capture structurally <90%. Manager can explain how portfolio limits downside and data confirms it. |
| 2–3 | Acceptable downside in some periods but no clear structural reason. Beta luck vs genuine risk awareness is ambiguous. |
| 0–1 | Downside capture exceeds benchmark in most stress windows. No evidence of deliberate drawdown management. |

---

## Pillar 7 — Parent (AMC Stewardship) (13 pts)

### Unitholder alignment / fee philosophy (5 pts)

| Score | When |
|---|---|
| 4–5 | TER decreases (or stays flat) as AUM grows. Direct plan supported without disproportionate incentivisation of Regular. No history of peak-cycle NFOs or reopening closed-ended schemes for fee income. |
| 2–3 | Neutral — TER hasn't moved much as AUM grew. No egregious abuses, no proactive alignment either. |
| 0–1 | TER maintained or raised as AUM grew. History of pushing Regular plans or launching thematic NFOs at market peaks. |

### Manager development and retention (3 pts)

| Score | When |
|---|---|
| 3 | Senior investment staff retained across cycles. Low fund-level manager turnover. Track record of promoting from within. |
| 1–2 | Average retention — some manager moves but not chronic. |
| 0 | High churn. Repeated external hires into senior roles. Track records frequently orphaned by departures. |

### Regulatory and compliance record (3 pts)

| Score | When |
|---|---|
| 3 | No material SEBI enforcement actions. Not implicated in front-running, distributor kickbacks, or dubious related-party transactions. |
| 1–2 | Minor SEBI observations; nothing material. |
| 0 | Material SEBI enforcement, front-running investigation, or governance failure in the Franklin/credit-episode fallout. |

### Business stability (2 pts)

| Score | When |
|---|---|
| 2 | AUM stable or growing. Parent-entity ownership stable. |
| 1 | AUM flat despite sector-wide growth; relative loss of investor confidence. |
| 0 | AUM declining, ownership-change uncertainty, or parent entity under financial stress. |

## Score line format

> Final score: 76/100 — Mandate 6/8 · Returns 11/15 · Risk-adj 15/20 · Portfolio 9/12 · People 13/17 · Process 11/15 · Parent 11/13 | **Silver** (Net alpha: positive; Conviction: High)

If Net Alpha Filter triggered a downgrade:
> Final score: 76/100 — ... | **Bronze** ↓ (Net alpha negative after 1.8% TER; downgraded from Silver)

## Category-specific scoring notes

| Category | Pillar adjustment |
|---|---|
| **Passive (index/ETF)** | Pillar 2 → Tracking Quality; Pillar 3 collapses to 12 pts (Pillar 2 absorbs 8); Pillar 4 collapses to 8 pts (replication fidelity only); Process = AMC's index-management and rebalancing infrastructure |
| **Debt** | Pillar 3 → risk-adjusted yield instead of Jensen's alpha; Pillar 4 → credit-quality breakup, duration discipline, issuer concentration, YTM context; Net Alpha Filter → yield-above-benchmark as numerator |
| **Hybrid** | Apply equity framework to equity portion, debt framework to debt portion, weighted by mandate allocation; People and Process must assess the asset-allocation call, not just stock/bond picking |

---

## Override clause

Score is a guide, not a sentence. If the score lands at, say, 74 (Silver) but the analysis surfaces a regulatory flag, major manager exit, or structural mandate change the rubric doesn't capture cleanly — the panel debate can downgrade. Document the override.

Low Conviction always caps the verdict at Bronze, regardless of numerical score.

---

## Sanity check

Before locking: would a thoughtful analyst put their own money here based on what was just written? If verdict and gut disagree, re-examine the driving pillar.
